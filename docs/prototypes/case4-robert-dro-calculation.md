## Step 8: DRO â€” Marital Share Calculation

### 8.1 DRO Division Rules

**Source:** RMC Â§18-408
- DRO divides benefit earned DURING the marriage WHILE employed
- Marital Share = (Service During Marriage / Total Service) Ã— Benefit
- Alternate Payee Share = Marital Share Ã— DRO Percentage

### 8.2 Service Period Analysis

**Total Employment Period:**
- Start: June 15, 1997
- End: March 31, 2026
- Total Service: 28.75 years (28 years, 9 months)

**Marriage Period:**
- Marriage: August 15, 1999
- Divorce: November 3, 2017
- Marriage Duration: 18 years, 2.5 months = 18.21 years

**Service DURING Marriage (while employed):**
- Robert was employed throughout the marriage (no gaps)
- Service during marriage: August 15, 1999 to November 3, 2017

**Calculation:**
- From August 15, 1999 to August 15, 2017 = 18 years exactly
- From August 15, 2017 to November 3, 2017 = 2 months, 19 days â‰ˆ 2.6 months

**Service During Marriage: 18 years, 3 months = 18.25 years**

### 8.3 Marital Fraction

**Marital Fraction = Service During Marriage / Total Service**

- Service During Marriage: 18.25 years
- Total Service: 28.75 years

**Marital Fraction = 18.25 / 28.75 = 0.6348 (63.48%)**

## Step 14: Alternative DRO Division Methods

### 14.1 This Case: Percentage of Marital Share

**Method:** 40% of the marital share (shared interest)
- Used in Case 4
- Patricia's share fluctuates with any benefit adjustments (COLA, etc.)
- Simpler to administer

### 14.2 Alternative: Fixed Dollar Amount

**Example:** DRO awards Patricia exactly $1,500/month
- Fixed amount regardless of Robert's final benefit
- Does not participate in COLAs
- Robert bears all risk/reward of benefit changes

### 14.3 Alternative: Separate Interest

**Method:** Patricia's share is severed at divorce
- Her share calculated as of divorce date
- She can elect her own retirement date
- Her share grows/shrinks based on her elections
- More complex but provides independence
