## Rate Component Definitions

### Health Care Trust Fund
Effective July 1, 2004, **1.02% of salary** is contributed by employers to the Health Care Trust Fund to pay health care premium subsidies to benefit recipients enrolled in the PERACare Health Benefits Program. This 1.02% is **included within** the employer base rates shown above — it is not an additional charge on top of the totals.

### AED — Amortization Equalization Disbursement
Additional employer contributions designed to reduce PERA's unfunded liability and amortization period. AED is calculated on the **total payroll** of PERA members and employees who can elect either PERA or another plan, regardless of which plan was elected (not just the payroll of DB plan members).

### SAED — Supplemental Amortization Equalization Disbursement
Additional employer contributions with the same purpose as AED. The SAED is, to the extent permitted by law, to be funded by moneys otherwise available for employee wage increases. **The SAED is not credited to member accounts.** Calculated on the same total payroll basis as AED.

### AED/SAED Change Triggers
- **State, School, and DPS Divisions:** Decreases mandated when the division's year-end funded status reaches 103%. Increases mandated when funded status reaches 103% and subsequently falls below 90%.
- **Local Government and Judicial Divisions:** Decreases mandated when the division's year-end funded status reaches 103%. Increases mandated when the trust fund reaches 90% funded status and subsequently falls below 90%.

### Automatic Adjustment Provision (AAP)
Under SB 18-200, member and employer contribution rates automatically adjust to ensure PERA can pay off its unfunded liability. Both member and employer rates can each increase or decrease by **up to 0.5% per year**, subject to statutory limits. Changes are determined each June and take effect the following January. The 1.00% shown in the Auto Adj column for all divisions reflects the current AAP increment.

### DC Supplement (State and Local Government Divisions only)
Beginning January 1, 2021, employer rates in the State and Local Government Divisions include a DC supplement. This is the employer contribution amount paid to PERA DC Plan participant accounts for members who began PERA membership on or after January 1, 2019, that would otherwise have gone to the DB trusts. The DC supplement is deposited into PERA's DB trusts to pay down unfunded liability. The amount is calculated each June by PERA's actuaries.

- DC Plan participants are not present in the School, DPS, or Judicial Divisions — no DC Supplement applies to those divisions.
- **State Division DC Plan participants:** 10.15% of salary deposited to their DC account from employer contributions; the remaining employer contributions are deposited in the State Division Trust Fund.
- **State Division Safety Officers in DC Plan:** 12.85% of salary deposited to their DC account; remainder to State Division Trust Fund.
- **Local Government Division DC Plan participants:** 10.00% of salary deposited to their DC account; remainder to Local Government Division Trust Fund.

## Safety Officer Definition

For the purposes of PERA benefits, "Safety Officers" include the following employees (note: defined in state statute as "State Troopers"):

- Peace officers with the Colorado State Patrol and Colorado Bureau of Investigation
- County sheriffs, undersheriffs, deputy sheriffs, noncertified deputy sheriffs, and detention officers in the **Local Government Division** whose PERA membership began on or after **January 1, 2020**
- Corrections officers classified as I through IV in the **State Division** whose PERA membership began on or after **January 1, 2020**
- Firefighters classified as I through VII employed with the Colorado Division of Fire Prevention and Control, beginning with service earned on or after **July 1, 2020**
- Wildlife officers and parks and recreation officers employed with the Colorado Division of Parks and Wildlife whose employment in these roles began on or after **January 1, 2011**, beginning with service earned on or after **July 1, 2023**
- County coroners and deputy coroners elected, reelected, or appointed by a Local Government Division employer on or after **January 1, 2021**, beginning with service earned on or after **January 1, 2025**
- Duly sworn employees of the Division of Fire Prevention and Control in the Department of Public Safety whose duties include structural or wildfire management, wildfire response, life-fire training, or wildfire leadership, beginning with service earned on or after **July 1, 2025**

## Contribution Rate Validation Rules (for Rules Engine)

The following rules are derived directly from the rate tables above and must be implemented as configurable, effective-dated rules — not hardcoded constants.

### Rate Lookup Key
Every contribution record must be validated against the matrix: **Division × Safety Officer classification × Effective date**

### Validation Logic
1. Identify the member's division from the member record
2. Identify whether the member qualifies as a Safety Officer per the membership-date and role criteria above
3. Look up the applicable employer rate components for the contribution period's effective date
4. Validate submitted contribution amounts against: member rate × salary and employer rate × salary for each component
5. AED and SAED are calculated on **total payroll** (including ORP-eligible employees), not just DB plan member payroll — flag any submission that applies AED/SAED only to DB member payroll

### Rate Table Must Be Versioned
Rates change annually each January (and mid-year for DPS when triggered by the five-year review). The rules engine must store rates with effective date ranges and select the correct rate for the payroll period being validated — not the current rate.

### Contribution Rate Limits (Statutory)
- Member rates cannot increase or decrease by more than **0.5% per year** under the AAP
- Employer rates cannot increase or decrease by more than **0.5% per year** under the AAP
- Statutory caps and floors on each rate component are established in C.R.S. Title 24, Article 51 — retrieve from statute before implementing upper/lower bound validation

## Notes for Claude Code Build Sessions

- **Do not hardcode any rate value.** Store all rates in a versioned reference table keyed by division, safety officer flag, and effective date range.
- **The January 2026 rates are current as of the date of this document.** Rates are re-published each January (and potentially mid-year for DPS). Before any build session that involves contribution validation, verify no rate update has been published since this document was retrieved.
- **Source for updates:** https://copera.org/employers/affiliating-with-pera/employer-contribution-rates or https://www.copera.org/pera-contribution-rates
- **School and DPS Divisions have no DC Supplement.** Do not add a DC Supplement column or validation for those divisions.
- **Judicial Division has no DC Supplement.** Same as above.
- **DPS employer rate is subject to bond payment variation.** The 18.40% total is the base — actual contributions may vary. Flag DPS employer submissions for this reason if the submitted amount differs from the table rate.
- **Local Government AED/SAED changed January 2026** — AED increased from 2.20% to 2.70%, SAED from 1.50% to 2.00%. Contribution files for periods before January 2026 must be validated against the 2025 rates, not the 2026 rates.
